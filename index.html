The Science

Why this works

The idea is simple: when children can picture an experience ahead of time, it stops feeling so unknown.

Read the story tonight — and tomorrow feels more familiar, more predictable, and easier to handle.

These stories are rooted in research used in education and therapy to reduce anxiety and build confidence.

Behind the Book

Created by a therapist.

I'm Kristina — a Board Certified Educational Therapist and a mom. This didn't start as a business. It started at home with my one-year-old.

I work from home, often in the evenings. And every time I went upstairs, she would fall apart when my husband stepped in. Even though she was safe, it felt like too much, too fast.

So I tried something I'd used for years with my students: a simple social story. We made a little book about what would happen.

Mommy gives a hug. Mommy goes upstairs. You can still hear her talking. Daddy stays with you. Then Mommy comes back.

We read it together — and everything changed. She wasn't surprised anymore. She knew what to expect. It felt familiar.

So we made another. One for her first airplane ride. One for the doctor. One for sleeping somewhere new.

Each time, the same thing happened: what felt big started to feel manageable. That's where Little Life Books began.

Kristina Ashburne
