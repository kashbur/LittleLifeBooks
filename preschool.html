Why this works

A young child's brain doesn't sharply separate a vivid imagined experience from a real one. Read this story tonight — and preschool already feels like somewhere they've been.

Inside the Book

Every page has a purpose.

Each page is built around a specific goal — reducing uncertainty, building familiarity, establishing routine, and anchoring the goodbye.

Reduces uncertainty

"This is Mia's school. It has a big yellow door and a classroom with butterflies painted on the wall."

Builds familiarity

"Mia's teacher is Ms. Rachel. She has curly hair and she's been looking forward to meeting Mia."

Establishes routine

"First Mia hangs her backpack on her hook. Then she finds her cubby. Then she goes to the morning table."

Anchors the goodbye

"And when the day is done — Mommy comes back. She always comes back. And Mia gets to tell her everything."

For most children, yes. Separation anxiety at drop-off is usually driven by uncertainty, not the separation itself. Children who can picture exactly what's coming — the classroom, the teacher, the goodbye, the pickup — show measurably lower stress at drop-off. It won't replace professional support when that's needed, but it's one of the most effective tools available.
Ideally 1–2 weeks before the first day. Repetition is what builds the familiarity — most families read it nightly. The night before school is especially important, so the experience is the last thing your child's brain rehearses before sleep.
Typically 2–5 years old. During the intake, you'll share your child's age and developmental stage — this significantly changes how the story is written. A story for a newly 2-year-old reads differently than one for a confident 4-year-old starting pre-K.
Social stories were originally developed for children with autism and remain one of the most evidence-based tools in autism support. For children with ADHD, sensory differences, or any other needs, we adjust the story accordingly. Share what you know about your child's nervous system in the intake, and we'll build around it.
